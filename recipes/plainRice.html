Plain Rice

Literally Just Rice

There are three basic kinds of rice: short-grain, long-grain, and medium-grain. Among them you'll find hundreds of different varieties. Short-grain rice is rounded and plump, with a high starch content that makes the grains stick together when cooked, especially if the rice has been milled to make white rice. Long-grain rice is much longer than it is wide. Its lower starch content makes the cooked grains lighter, dryer, and more easily separated. Medium-grain rice slots right between short- and long-grain rice both in shape and starchiness.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Water

Steps

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a small saucepan with a tight-fitting lid.
  2. Add 1 cup of rice, stir briefly to break up clumps of rice.
  3. Cover the pot with the lid and reduce the heat to its lowest setting for 20 minutes.
  4. Turn off the burner and remove the pan from the heat. Let the rice sit, covered, for an additional five minutes.
